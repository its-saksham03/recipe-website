Classic Pasta Carbonara

Non-Veg Easy
10
Prep (min)
20
Cook (min)
30
Total (min)
2
Servings

Pasta Carbonara is a timeless Roman dish where hot pasta is tossed with a rich, creamy sauce made from eggs, Pecorino Romano, and crispy guanciale. No cream needed — just the magic of heat and technique.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 200g spaghetti or rigatoni
  • 100g guanciale or pancetta, diced
  • 2 large eggs + 1 egg yolk
  • 50g Pecorino Romano, grated
  • 50g Parmesan cheese, grated
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt (for pasta water)
  • 1 clove garlic (optional)

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Boil the pasta. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water before draining.
  2. Fry the guanciale. In a cold pan, add diced guanciale. Cook over medium heat until crispy and golden. Add garlic if using, cook 1 minute, then remove.
  3. Make the egg mixture. In a bowl, whisk eggs, egg yolk, Pecorino, and Parmesan. Season generously with black pepper. This is your sauce.
  4. Combine pasta and guanciale. Add drained pasta to the pan with guanciale (off heat). Toss well so pasta absorbs the fat.
  5. Add the egg sauce. Remove pan from heat. Pour in the egg mixture and toss quickly. Add pasta water a splash at a time, tossing constantly, until you get a creamy coating sauce.
  6. Serve immediately. Plate up and finish with extra Parmesan and cracked black pepper.
