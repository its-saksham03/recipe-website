Hyderabadi Chicken Biryani

Non-Veg Hard
30
Prep (min)
60
Cook (min)
90
Total (min)
4
Servings

The king of rice dishes. Authentic Hyderabadi Dum Biryani — marinated chicken layered with partially cooked basmati rice and slow-cooked to perfection. The saffron aroma is absolutely unforgettable.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 750g chicken pieces, bone-in
  • 2 cups basmati rice, soaked 30 min
  • 1 cup thick yoghurt
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • Whole spices: bay leaf, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon
  • 2 tsp biryani masala
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chilli powder
  • Pinch of saffron in 3 tbsp warm milk
  • Fresh mint and coriander leaves
  • 3 tbsp ghee + oil for frying
  • Salt to taste

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Fry the onions. Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pan. Fry sliced onions on medium-high until deep golden and crispy (about 20 min). Drain on paper towels. These are your "barista" onions.
  2. Marinate the chicken. Mix yoghurt, ginger-garlic paste, biryani masala, turmeric, red chilli, salt, and half the fried onions. Coat chicken and marinate for at least 1 hour.
  3. Par-cook the rice. Boil salted water with whole spices. Add soaked rice and cook until 70% done (white dot in centre). Drain immediately.
  4. Cook the chicken gravy. Heat ghee in the pot, add whole spices, then the marinated chicken. Cook on medium heat for 15–20 minutes until 90% cooked and gravy is thick.
  5. Layer the biryani. Spread chicken and gravy evenly. Add the rice on top. Drizzle saffron milk, scatter remaining fried onions, mint, coriander, and ghee.
  6. Dum cooking. Cover tightly. Cook on high 5 min, then lowest heat for 25 min. Rest 10 min before opening.
  7. Serve. Gently mix from the bottom up. Serve hot with raita.
