Restaurant-Style Dal Tadka

Vegetarian Easy
10
Prep (min)
35
Cook (min)
45
Total (min)
3–4
Servings

Creamy yellow lentils tempered with a blazing hot mix of butter, garlic, and dried red chillies. The soul of Indian comfort food — serve with hot rotis or steamed rice.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  • ¼ cup chana dal
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 4–5 garlic cloves, sliced (tadka)
  • 2 dried red chillies
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • ½ tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • ½ tsp red chilli powder
  • 2 tbsp ghee + 1 tbsp oil
  • Fresh coriander to garnish
  • Salt to taste

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Pressure cook the dal. Rinse both dals. Pressure cook with 3 cups water, turmeric, and salt for 4–5 whistles until soft and mushy. Mash lightly.
  2. Prepare the base. Heat oil, add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add onions and sauté until golden. Add ginger-garlic paste, cook 2 minutes.
  3. Add tomatoes and spices. Add chopped tomatoes, coriander powder, red chilli powder. Cook until tomatoes break down and oil separates (about 8 min).
  4. Combine dal and masala. Add the cooked dal to the masala. Stir and adjust water to your preferred consistency. Simmer 10 minutes. Adjust salt.
  5. Make the tadka. Heat ghee in a small pan until very hot. Add sliced garlic and fry until golden. Add mustard seeds, dried red chillies, and a pinch of red chilli powder. Sizzle 10 seconds.
  6. Serve. Pour the hot tadka over the dal. Garnish with coriander and serve with rice or rotis.
